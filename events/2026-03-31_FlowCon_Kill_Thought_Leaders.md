# FlowCon 2026 - First kill all the Thought Leaders

## First kill all the Thought Leaders

*Chris Matts*

Chris argues that AI adoption is stuck because we're listening to the wrong people. Thought Leaders sell solutions to problems they don't have — what organisations actually need are practitioners who own the problem and can develop new practices from within.

### The value stack

Value flows from the practitioner outwards, not the other way around:

> practitioner with a problem > practitioner with tools > organisation

💡 a tool without a problem or a practice produces no value.

**AI today is mostly tools without problems or practices** — which is why so much of it fails to land.

### Maturity Mapping = Social Practice Theory

Maturity Mapping (a Wardley-adjacent technique) is essentially Social Practice Theory applied to organisations:
 - practices emerge from practitioners
 - evolve through use
 - and only later get codified.

### Crossing the Chasm — revisited

Reference: Geoffrey Moore, *Crossing the Chasm*.

The chasm between **early adopters** and the **early majority** isn't a marketing gap — it's the gap between two distinct communities:

| Community of **Needs** | Community of **Solutions**         |
|------------------------|------------------------------------|
| Trailblazers           | Thought Leaders                    |
| Have the problem       | Have the answer (or claim to)      |
| Develop practices      | Package, evangelise, sell          |

💡 the work happens in the community of needs. The community of solutions monetises it afterwards — and often distorts it.

### Why kill the Thought Leaders?

On AI specifically, Thought Leaders are actively harmful:
- they delay the learning of practitioner communities
- they push tools ahead of problems and practices
- they replace collective sensemaking with packaged answers

⚠️ "Killing" the Thought Leaders means *elevating the Trailblazers* — giving oxygen to people actually wrestling with the problem, not the people selling slides about it.

Cross-reference: Romeu Moura's *Rinse-less brushing* talk at the same conference — same underlying tension between descriptivism (what practitioners do) and prescriptivism (what Thought Leaders say they should do).

The *Listerine* example from Romeu illustrates the same dynamic: a problem (bad breath) invented to justify a product.

### Indicators that matter to Execs

Most tech investment discussions use the wrong metrics. Two that actually work:

- **Cost of Delay** — measures the efficiency of turning ideas and concepts into delivered value
- **Investment Duration** — time from start of development to cash returned

These reframe tech spend as an investment decision rather than a cost line.

### What to build instead

> Create a network of people that facilitates value propagation.

Not a centre of excellence. Not a thought-leadership function. A network where practitioners with problems can find each other and spread practices horizontally.
